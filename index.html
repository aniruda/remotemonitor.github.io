Project Description-

The future of agriculture would be protected cultivation which is happening due to climate changes forcing farmers and growers to cultivate and grow the crops inside the polyhouse. This protected cultivation would use technology as the best tool for crop management and monitoring.

About my Project and Client-

I have chosen Mr Keshav as the client as he is setting up a hydroponic farm in a controlled environment for cultivation of leafy greens at Brijwasan. This farm will grow a variety of exotic leafy green like lettuce, ice burg green, Lolo roso, Kale, Rocket lettuce etc. This polyhouse is under construction as you can see on the background. This project has a polyhouse structure,It is made out of Galvanised Iron Structure and polyfilm and has a NFT (Nutrient Film Technique) which is a channel for growing plants through water mixed with nutrients. It also has a small command centre built outside which will help control various automated systems in polyhouse such as Fan pads and Foggers which will help maintain the right temperature and humidity for the plants. The command centre will also help maintain the sensors and display data from it to be sent to the cloud server.There is a crop cycle of 45 days which means you can have 7 to 8 cycles of crops which is not possible in normal agriculture.
